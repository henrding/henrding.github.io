Sunday Morning

On the couch that is colored brown,
a boy named George starts to lie down.
With no planned agenda in sight,
he longs to watch T.V ‘til night.

Grabbing the remote and turning it on,
while his dog stares from the grassy lawn,
he flips through the channels one by one
as the roof above shields him from the sun.

Click until he has arrived at his favorite show,
as children on the T.V shout “Joe Schmo!”
George relaxes as he sits down on his behind,
with no worries in his adolescent mind.

He sinks into his seat, slouchy and slow,
as his brain buries into a slumber of low.
His mom walks into the sleeping den to bid
wishing that she could go back as a kid.

The Other Side

Waiting for his Princess on the other side of the world,
the man longs for a partner whom he can kiss
while the sun rises above the tranquil sea.
Peonies blossom and tulips bloom with grace,
pouring through every crevasse of the land like the desires in his heart.
Picking up the ruby red rose,
smelling the scent of irresistible romance,
shutting his lustful eyes,
he transcends into a magical fantasy of sensation and beauty.

He dreams,
“of.….
...love”
, she dreams.

She stares up at the shimmering night sky,
catching a shooting star in the distance.
Closing her delighted eyes,
ones that sparkle bubbles of champagne,
taking a deep breath of the cold breeze,
she makes a wish for a passionate lover.
While the sun sets into a peaceful sleep,
the woman waits for him to rescue her from episodes of loneliness,
as her Prince Charming wakes up on the other side of the world.

Dear Shrek

You live a life of wonder;
it really makes me ponder
since your spectacular story
is filled with glitz and glory.

You taught to never judge a book by its cover
and seek personality over looks in a lover.
Oh how thankful we are for your bravery
as you are for a feast that is savory.

You prove that you are not a betrayer
by relating to an onion with many layers -
a metaphor that touches our hearts
in a way that will never grow apart.

You have a loving wife
whom you would save under a knife,
What an ideal relationship goal -
a companionship without any holes.

Your actions prove you to be
a man of gallant glee.
Your acts of kindness stand out
as you do not pretend or pout.

You provided our lives with laughter,
from Shrek I to Forever After.
I wish I could shake your hand,
but I would have to travel to your land.

You are never, ever stingy
with your companions like Gingy
By defeating Lord Farquaad,
you are very much our God.

And so I leave with a final thought -
I thank you for lots and lots
as your life is filled with sublime
once upon a Shrek time.

The Black Box That Swings

The girl tenses
as her arms grasp onto the metal chains,
while she swings back into depression
with the black box that confines her.

Black above the girl,
like a dark ceiling
hanging under gloomy clouds,
shielding her from the sun,
as it sucks the life out of her,
breath by breath.

Black below the girl,
like a marble tiled floor
that divides her from the ground,
having no foundation
to help her reach for the skies.

Black to the left, right, front, back of the girl,
like four walls
preventing her from escaping the nightmare,
as she is trapped
inside the imaginary black box.

Whoosh!

The girl returns back to reality
as she sways back and forth
on the swing set,
reassured that she is free from terror.

Letting go of her loath,
flying towards freedom,
and screaming with satisfaction,
she escapes the imaginary black box
for a mere minute.

But as she returns back to the ground
with the euphoria long gone,
the girl starts to regret her life,
tumbling into a deep haze of horror.

The girl tenses
as her arms grasp onto the metal chains,
while she swings back into depression
with the black box that confines her.

~ The Finish Line ~

From the transparent skies,
from the harp of sound,
from the gift of light,
we are given a life to explore.
Take in every breath,
Take in every breath,
savor every dish,
taste every snowflake that falls down
on a cold winter’s night.
Life is a symphony of quiet and mystery -
quiet like the wind breeze,
a deafening silence that you can see;
mystery like the tranquil poke of a needle
as it sends the patient to a deep doze.
But light exchanges with dark -
darkness that swells inside the heart
like a balloon ready to pop;
it pops with hatred and shame.
Yet we can all make the most -
the most out of life,
the most out of a week,
the most out of a day,
the most out of every second.
One day,
our existence will be nothing
but a glimpse of
memory within someone’s brain.

It is time to take control of our lives
and seek what we desire
as we draw closer to the finish line.
How we make the most of our time
is in our power -
go on that walk with your family,
tell them you love them,
visit the Seven Wonders,
Life is harmonious,
with happy segments of laugh,
yet episodes of grief.
Today we can turn the end
into a new beginning --
beginnings filled with memories.
Turn the sadness into happiness,
turn the anger into smiles,
turn the hate into love.
As we reflect the moon’s darkness,
transform it into a mystical fantasy of light.
Shine the world with one’s legacy
as we glow with the sparks that carry us.
Carrying sparks,
carrying stories,
carrying emotions,
we race to the finish line.
